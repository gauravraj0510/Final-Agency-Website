So you’re building an **AI consultancy that diagnoses companies and then sells them automation**. Basically a doctor that politely tells businesses their processes are a mess and then sells them surgery. Sensible model. Humans love hearing they’re inefficient as long as there’s a shiny dashboard involved.

The trick with your **10–12 question funnel** is simple:
You’re not collecting data. You’re **engineering the moment where the client realizes they’re losing money**. That realization sells the automation.

Let’s structure this properly so the LLM can actually produce meaningful insight instead of motivational nonsense.

---

# 1. The Core Framework (How the Funnel Thinks)

Your diagnostic should evaluate **5 dimensions of a business**.

**1. Revenue Engine**
How they acquire and convert customers.

**2. Operations**
How work actually gets done internally.

**3. Data & Visibility**
Whether decisions are based on data or vibes.

**4. Technology Stack**
Tools, integrations, fragmentation.

**5. Scalability Risk**
What breaks if they grow 3×.

Think of it like a **mini McKinsey audit but done by an LLM in 90 seconds**.

---

# 2. Categories of Questions

Your 10–12 questions should be distributed like this:

| Category        | Questions | Purpose                     |
| --------------- | --------- | --------------------------- |
| Business Basics | 2         | Context                     |
| Revenue Flow    | 2         | Identify growth bottlenecks |
| Operations      | 3         | Automation opportunities    |
| Technology      | 2         | Integration opportunities   |
| Data            | 1         | Intelligence gaps           |
| Scaling         | 1–2       | Future problems             |

---

# 3. The Actual 12 Question Structure

## 1. Business Context

**Q1**
What industry is your business in?

Examples:
Healthcare / Real Estate / Ecommerce / Manufacturing / Services

**Q2**
Company size?

* Solo / Founder
* 2–10 employees
* 10–50
* 50+

Why this matters:
Automation ROI depends on scale.

---

## 2. Revenue Engine

**Q3**
How do most of your customers currently find you?

Options:

* Referrals
* Paid ads
* Social media
* Outbound sales
* Marketplaces
* Other

**Q4**
What happens after a lead shows interest?

Example answers:

* Manual calls
* WhatsApp follow-ups
* CRM pipeline
* Email automation
* No structured process

This reveals **sales automation potential**.

---

## 3. Operations

This is where the gold is.

**Q5**
What are the 3 most repetitive tasks your team performs every day?

Examples:

* Data entry
* Customer support replies
* Lead qualification
* Report generation
* Inventory tracking

**Q6**
How many hours per week are spent on manual administrative work?

* <5 hours
* 5–20 hours
* 20–50 hours
* 50+ hours

**Q7**
What processes cause the most delays or mistakes?

Examples:

* Customer onboarding
* Order processing
* Reporting
* Internal approvals
* Scheduling

This tells the LLM **where to automate first**.

---

## 4. Technology Stack

**Q8**
Which tools do you currently use?

Examples:

* Excel / Google Sheets
* CRM (HubSpot, Zoho, Salesforce)
* ERP
* WhatsApp
* Email
* Custom software

**Q9**
Are these systems integrated with each other?

* Fully integrated
* Partially integrated
* Mostly manual
* No integration

Translation:
“Do your systems talk to each other or hate each other?”

---

## 5. Data & Decision Making

**Q10**
How do you currently track business performance?

Options:

* Dashboard / BI tools
* CRM reports
* Manual spreadsheets
* We don't track much

This reveals **analytics opportunity**.

---

## 6. Scalability

**Q11**
If your business suddenly got **3× more customers**, what would break?

Examples:

* Customer support
* Fulfillment
* Sales follow-ups
* Reporting
* Inventory

This reveals **future automation opportunity**.

---

## 7. Strategic Priority

**Q12**
What is your biggest priority right now?

* Increase revenue
* Reduce operational cost
* Improve efficiency
* Better customer experience
* Scale faster

---

# 4. What the LLM Should Output

The AI shouldn't give a novel. Humans don't read.

It should produce **3 sections**.

### 1. Operational Diagnosis

Example:

> Your business currently relies heavily on manual processes in lead follow-ups and internal reporting. This creates delays in customer conversion and operational inefficiency.

---

### 2. Key Automation Opportunities

Example:

**Opportunity 1 — Lead Qualification AI**

Automate lead filtering and follow-ups using AI agents integrated with WhatsApp or email.

Expected impact:

* 40–60% faster lead response
* 20–30% increase in conversions

---

**Opportunity 2 — Internal Workflow Automation**

Automate repetitive tasks such as reporting, approvals, and onboarding.

Expected impact:

* Save 15–25 hours per week.

---

### 3. Automation Maturity Score

Score them across 5 dimensions:

| Area                  | Score |
| --------------------- | ----- |
| Revenue Automation    | 3/10  |
| Operations Automation | 2/10  |
| Data Intelligence     | 4/10  |
| Systems Integration   | 3/10  |
| Scalability           | 4/10  |

Then:

**Overall Automation Readiness Score: 3.2 / 10**

Add **one brutal question** near the end.

Example:

That answer exposes the **single biggest automation opportunity**.
